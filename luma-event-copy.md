## Workshop 1

Title: Hermes + You.com: Ship a Governed Account-Brief Workflow

Date and time: [DATE TBD], [TIME TBD] PT

Short description:

Build a reusable account-brief workflow that a non-technical user can run, an admin can govern, and a team can pilot in production, with an optional NemoClaw review layer for legal, compliance, approved-language, and capability-governance checks.

Luma description:

Join a public, hands-on Hermes + You.com workshop on building a governed account-brief workflow for customers, prospects, partners, and companies.

We will start by running the workflow: ask Hermes for a source-grounded account brief, use You.com web intelligence to gather current context, inspect the sources, refine the output, and turn the successful pattern into something reusable.

The workshop is designed for product, GTM, solutions, legal, compliance, and operations teams that want useful AI workflows without losing control over data, credentials, source quality, approved language, tool access, or human review.

By the end, attendees will leave with a production-ready workflow card that defines the owner, trigger, inputs, output format, review rules, success metrics, and a two-week pilot plan.

What we will cover:
- Run an account-brief workflow with Hermes + You.com.
- Support non-technical users with a repeatable ask, inspect, refine, reuse pattern.
- Decide who can use the workflow, which You.com tools are allowed, where credentials live, what data is off-limits, and what requires review.
- Add NemoClaw as a governance pass for unsupported claims, sensitive language, external-use risk, approved wording, and tool-permission concerns.
- Convert the successful prompt into a reusable Hermes skill or playbook.
- Define a two-week pilot plan that an admin and team owner can govern.

Who should attend:
- GTM, sales, customer success, partnerships, and marketing teams that need better account context.
- Product and solutions teams designing governed AI workflows.
- Legal and compliance partners who want repeatable review rules instead of one-off AI approvals.
- Admins and technical operators responsible for tool access, credentials, and review rules.

Takeaway:

You will leave with a working blueprint for a reusable `/account-brief` workflow and a pilot plan you can adapt for your own team.

## Workshop 2

Title: You.com Answers API: Build a Trusted Answer Experience

Date and time: [DATE TBD], [TIME TBD] PT

Short description:

Design a fast, source-backed answer experience around You.com's upcoming Answers API, with trust patterns for citations, freshness, uncertainty, escalation, and NemoClaw-powered risk review.

Luma description:

This public workshop focuses on the product pattern behind a trusted answer experience: how a user question becomes a concise answer with sources, follow-up questions, and a clear path to deeper research.

We will position the upcoming Answers API relative to You.com's existing Search, Contents, and Research APIs, then use the session to collect feedback on messaging, response format, documentation needs, SDK expectations, pricing questions, demo queries, and launch positioning.

The goal is not just to show an API. The goal is to design the experience around it: what users see, when the system should show uncertainty, how citations should appear, when raw search results are enough, when NemoClaw should add approved language, risk classification, or capability-governance checks, and when the product should escalate to deeper research or human review.

What we will cover:
- Clarify where Answers API fits alongside Search, Contents, and Research.
- Turn a user question into a concise, source-backed answer.
- Design source display, freshness signals, uncertainty language, and follow-up questions.
- Add a NemoClaw risk pass for legal, compliance, privacy, contract, regulated-industry, externally actionable, or high-risk agent answers.
- Compare raw search results with synthesized answers.
- Build a lightweight quality scorecard for correctness, citation support, latency, freshness, formatting, and failure handling.
- Capture launch feedback for docs, SDKs, pricing, demos, and positioning.

Who should attend:
- Product managers and designers building answer, search, support, research, or assistant experiences.
- Developers and solutions teams evaluating You.com APIs.
- GTM and developer-relations teams shaping launch messaging and demos.

Takeaway:

You will leave with a clear product pattern for a trusted answer experience and a practical scorecard for evaluating answer quality.
